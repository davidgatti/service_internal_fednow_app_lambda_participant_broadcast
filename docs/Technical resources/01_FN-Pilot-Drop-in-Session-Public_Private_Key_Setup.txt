Anti-Trust Compliance
• Pilot participants are expected to ensure that their participation and communications at the pilot
engagements and related meetings do not violate antitrust laws.

• This means that no activity or discussion at engagements or other related functions may be
engaged in for the purpose of bringing about any understanding or agreement among participants
to do any of the following: (a) raise, stabilize, or set future prices; (b) regulate future production
levels among individual participants; (c) allocate geographic markets or customers; (d) encourage
boycotts or seek to exclude specific participants; or (e) aid in creating improper monopolies.

• In addition, participants should avoid discussing or revealing any individual participant’s
competitively sensitive information, including any participant’s prices, discounts, costs, capacity,
inventory, sales, future business plans, or bids for contracts.

• Any questions regarding the meaning or applicability of this anti-trust statement, as well as any
concerns regarding activities or discussions at engagements sessions or other FedNow meetings,
should be promptly brought to the attention of the Federal Reserve Bank presenter of the meeting
or working group session.

Message Signing and Key Management
• Messages exchanged with participants will be cryptographically signed:
• Digitally signing messages has several benefits, mainly providing a non-repudiation
means to reduce fraudulent messaging.
• Participants will use key pairs to sign messages (not relying on X.509 certificates or
certificate authorities for message signatures):
• Participants will create their own key pairs (public and private) with specifications
defined by the FedNow Service.
• Participants will provide the public key to the FedNow Service over a secure channel.
• Participants will be able to programmatically manage keys using APIs provided by the
FedNow Service.
• Participants will sign all messages with their private key and provide a reference to the
FedNow Service of which key was used.
• Message signing keys are valid up to a year.
• Participants are encouraged to have multiple, active keys with overlapping time periods.

Message Signing Summary
• The FedNow Service uses Simple Key Pairs (not certificates) to sign
messages.
• Participants utilize the FedNow interface to initialize their first key.
• Participants can then use MQ messaging to add/revoke keys.
• Keys have a maximum lifespan of 365 days.
• The FedNow Service will regularly rotate its own keys and notify
participants through an MQ message.
• Participants can request to see all FedNow Keys through the FedNow
interface and through MQ calls at any time.
• All FedNow messages to participants will be signed.
